ISLT 9450: Research Methods in ISLT

Course Description: Research methods and ethics of research in the social sciences, focusing on the information professions and learning technologies.

I am glad I took this course in the latter half of my degree program. Prior to it, the coursework in other classes had given me new interests and ideas to explore. This course was the opportunity for that exploration, at least at the initial stage. Though I have conducted research in the humanities before, it was interesting to see new methods of research in this class.

I also really enjoyed the peer review process and opportunities to gain formative feedback on my research proposal at multiple stages of the writing journey â€“ from exploring my own epistemology to understanding the institutional review process. This course prepared me for expanded work and research, both in my planned doctoral degree and on the job. I was able to identify a real and pressing issue in my own field and explore possible solutions. Lastly, this course offered the opportunity to better my own academic writing and to respect that process!
