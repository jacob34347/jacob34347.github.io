Teaching and Technology

The field of education has played a central role in my life since childhood. In elementary school my parents bought me a small easel and chalkboard as a gift, and my first instinct was to set up a classroom in our living room and teach them about the moon using powdered donuts and rudimentary drawings. Later in high school I looked to my Latin teacher as a role model and pictured myself in his shoes, teaching a class and engaging with learners. In short, it was never a question for me if I would eventually join the ranks of educators, but when and at what capacity.

In college I acted towards my goals by dual majoring in History Education and German. After graduation, I received a Fulbright Scholarship to Germany, where I decided to pivot into the field of German education, and, following this, pursued my Masters in German Studies. Since earning my Master's degree, I have served as a German teacher in both middle and high schools, and I have truly enjoyed it. In recent years, I have considered where I might go next in the field of education, and whether I should work towards a more advanced degree in curriculum design, or technology, or even school leadership. However, this line of thinking coincided with something else happening in my own department, which has helped to clarify my direction. Simply put, I have become the de facto tech expert amongst my colleagues and hardly a day goes by where I am not assisting with technological issues or sharing my own integrations of technology into my curriculum and instruction. In truth, this has been a nice experience for me. Many of my hobbies are tied to technology in some way, and I have always had a relatively solid understanding of technology and its uses.

I decided to study in the Educational Specialist degree program in Learning Technologies and Design because it will pave the path forward for me. It allows me to create a more relevant and effective classroom environment for my students right now. We are in a rapidly developing informational age, and technology stands to add high-effect value to my classroom instruction. Our students engage with numerous technologies daily and their literacy in this field is central to their future successes. Enriching instruction with technology affords students more valuable experience with these skills, in addition to creating a more effective learning environment.

Outside of the classroom, this program allows me to better serve as a resource for my colleagues, and a leader in our department. I want to be able to bring new ideas and implementations of new learning platforms to our World Language department. Our instruction has already evolved considerably in the past few years alone with virtual learning and newer, more dynamic learning management systems, and I want to see what else is on the horizon and how I can help to facilitate more positive change. Lastly, this program creates new opportunities for me. Currently, I plan to use this degree in my present capacity as a teacher. However, it also opens new doors for me in instructional coaching and technology facilitator roles in my district, and even lays the conceptual groundwork for my planned Ed.D. studies and the types of research I will want to pursue in the future. I have a passion not only for education, but also for the exciting world of technology, and for what it can bring to those I teach and work with.
