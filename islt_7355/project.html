Competency 1: Design and Development of Learning Systems

Met by: Teaching Concept Video in Intro to Digital Media (ISLT 7361)


Description: This was the second large project in my Intro to Digital Media course. We had to create a video that taught a concept and brought together multiple forms of digital media: image, audio, and video. As a ski instructor, I chose to distill an advanced concept (angulation) by introducing it and making it tangible through dry practice. This project was a piece of a larger website that I created for the course on the topic of “angulation”. This website was also hosted in a VR space, so that classmates could explore each other's websites and learn about new concepts.

Reflection: This project demonstrates my ability to design and develop a virtual space that leverages various digital media to teach a concept. Specifically, the learning experience (i.e. website) utilizes new, effective media and is grounded in principles of design and aesthetics. Each shot was planned and storyboarded to support these design principles.

Competency 2: Front-End Analysis and System Evaluation

Met by: Usability Report in Intro to Digital Media (ISLT 7361)


Link to Project

Description: This was the final graduate project in my Intro to Digital Media course. This Usability and Learning Evaluation Report was designed for us to see our learning experience website through another's eyes, which led to re-evaluation and re-design of the website. Four different users accessed my website (teaching the concept of “angulation” in skiing) and filled out different reports on their experiences. I observed Participant 1 while they used my website and asked them to perform different tasks to monitor usability. Participants 2 and 3 accessed the website on their own and filled out surveys. Lastly, Participant 4 evaluated the didactic and pedagogical aspects of the website for effectiveness. Their answers yielded a large amount of data and reshaped the website.

Reflection: This project demonstrates my ability to not only gather and analyze data, but also apply the findings to strengthen the design and development of my digital learning experience website. After receiving the user responses in my report, I revisited my website and made edits to key design elements to create a more accessible and user-friendly experience.

Competency 3: Leadership and Social Responsibility

Met by: Presentation on Innovative Technologies in Technology to Enhance Learning (ISLT 9467)


Link to Project

Description: This project required researching a new, innovative learning technology and creating a presentation of my findings. We were also required to evaluate benefits and drawbacks of this technology for current and future use. Ultimately, this presentation was recorded as a video and uploaded using VoiceThread. I chose to research virtual reality, in particular a few programs that could enrich learning in the classroom: TiltBrush, Wander, and ImmerseMe. These applications for VR headsets stand to strongly enhance learning in the classroom, but raised a number of ethical questions and considerations, particularly in regard to their accessibility and affordability for all students.

Reflection: This project demonstrates my ability to evaluate and understand the responsibilities of a leader in technology pertaining to ethics. In this project I identified issues with affordability with VR technology, as well as failures to make the technology accessible for all learners, especially those with disabilities. I also explored the lack of standards for manufacturers and developers, which have led to many of the accessibility problems with the hardware and software.
